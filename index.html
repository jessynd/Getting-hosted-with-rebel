##Understanding servers - We build websites on our computers. All our website files go inside our folder. - To put that website "online", all we need to do is move our folder to a server, or host.
##What is a server? - A server is a computer, just like our laptops, but they never turn off and they are always connected to the internet. - Hosts own many different servers, and their job is to make sure that servers run properly so that our websites are always available.

How do we get our files to the server?

It's easy! Almost as easy as dragging a file from one folder to another.

  1. First, we get a server and a domain. Also known as a host. Today we'll be using Rebel.
  2. Second, we get an FTP client.
  3. We use our FTP client to upload files to our server.
##How to get a server and domain - You may purchase space on a server from a host. - Many hosts exist but today we will be using Rebel who has generously donated free hosting and domains for everyone.
##What's the difference between a server and a domain? - A server is the computer that will "host" the folder with our files. - The domain is the internet address that people will have to use to access that folder on the server. - Though most hosts will also sell you domains, you can purchase these separately and then connect them through your hosting provider. - So for example, if you've purchased a domain on GoDaddy, but would like to host your website on Rebel, don't worry! You can easily transfer your domain to Rebel, or "point" it to the rebel server. - Pointing a domain means that when someone goes to your website, GoDaddy will redirect that address to your Rebel-hosted website. No one will tell the difference.
##Setting up with Rebel 1. Go to www.rebel.com and select web hosting under Email & Hosting in the top right navigation 2. Choose ‘THE IDOL’ hosting plan 3. Search for your desired domain in the pop up that appears 4. Once you’ve selected an available domain you will be directed to a login page with a green bar indicating your hosting is ready to go. Login or follow the steps to create a new free account and click “complete registration”. 5. Once you have completed your registration, go to your shopping cart (icon in the top right corner of your page). 6. Enter the promo code LLCHACK the ‘Add a Promo Code’ box in the upper right corner. Click the orange ‘Apply’ button. Your total charge will then be changed to $0. 7. Agree to the Terms and Conditions and select ‘Process Order’ 8. Welcome to Rebel!

Managing your hosting on Rebel

  1. Login into your rebel account.

  2. Select Manage Hosting

  3. Select C-Panel for your domain

  4. You could upload directly inside the C-Panel by using the File Manager.

    Go to Tools>File Manager

    Then select the "public" folder.

  5. Once in the public folder, use the upload box to select the files you'd like to upload.

    And that's it! Your website is live.

But there's a better way!

FTP

File Transfer Protocol

Uploading all your files one by one is tedious.

Instead, we can use FTP to upload many files at once, and do so without having to login to our host (Rebel) all the time.

  1. To use FTP, we will need an FTP client. A piece of software that allows us to connect to the server and upload files.

    Before we get to the FTP client, since we're already logged into our hosting provider, let's get our FTP credentials to use later.

    In your C-Panel go to the FTP section and click on "Show Users".

    Click on the edit button next to your domain.

  2. Create a password for your ftp account. Make sure it has capital letters and numbers.

    Now, we're ready to connect to our server using an FTP client.

    Don't forget that password!

The FTP Client

So many clients

There are many FTP clients available out there.

"Client" just means "a program that allows you to connect to your server and upload folders"

Today we will use Cyberduck as our fTP client.

If you have not downloaded it yet, please go here

Connecting to our Server with Cyberduck

  1. Open Cyberduck

  2. Connect to Server

  3. Select the folder to upload to

  4. Choose to upload files

  5. Select your files

  6. Watch them load

  7. Go visit your new website that is live!!!
